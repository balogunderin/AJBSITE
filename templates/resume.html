Bio

I am a champion for youth development and a passionate social entrepreneur at the intersection of education and strategic human resources. Simply put, I am a gardener of young minds, cultivating potential and grooming individuals with skills for transforming Africa’s workforce.

My career journey started as an intern at the Ford Foundation and the United Nations Development Programme, where a role was created for me at a time where I imagined the world to be my oyster.

Currently, I lead as Country Manager Nigeria for the Power Learn Project Africa, a tech start-up with an objective to train one million software developers across Africa, brewed in Kenya and now maturing in 4 other countries. Here I foster a collaborative and inclusive learning environment to nurture the tech talent pipeline.

I add value as an Employee Experience Architect and co-founder at Jobred, a pioneering job centre and employability skills toolkit created to bridge the gap between education and employment. I do so through "I am talent," and ‘’12 work talents’’ modules of self- discovery in one’s authentic self, designed to help individuals identify their unique strengths to market themselves to potential employers.

I volunteer at the United Nations Committee on the Status of Women (UNCSW) under the auspices of International Business & Professional Women and sit on the board of Unites Cisco Academy (Tech for schools).

Personal mission : I am driven to create a future where every African youth has access to meaningful employment and economic opportunities

My Beliefs: I believe my purpose is to help others find theirs and this drives me to continue to implement innovative initiatives that prioritize social responsibility. I also believe our continent is our oyster, and that we can have the Africa we all want to see and so truly deserve.

My loves: Family, walking barefeet in my guava and avocado garden as well as tennis, jogging and yoga

My hates: being told ‘I don’t know’ in an AI enabled world

Fun facts: I was born in the Buea Mountains of Cameroon. I smile to remember a childhood in Victoria where I climbed and fell out of guava trees, got bitten by gigantic ants on mango trees because my sister and I had to make endless bottles of mango jam; when we picked seashells, swam in the ocean and fished for tiny fish with large plastic bowls, where life was simple before adulthood imposed itself on us

Why the basketball photograph?: In my role at Power Learn Project Africa, I developed a ‘’Tech meets Basketball’’ concept to foster engagement and encourage girls into tech through a partnership with @HoopDreamsNG. It was taken during a photoshoot celebrating that partnership. Photo courtesy of @moyin_obey
